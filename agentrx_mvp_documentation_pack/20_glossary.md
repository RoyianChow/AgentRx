# Glossary

## Agent-Native
A system designed around multiple AI agents performing specialized workflow tasks, rather than adding one chatbot to an existing app.

## Prescription Intake
The process of receiving a prescription document or e-prescription and creating a workflow record.

## OCR
Optical Character Recognition. Converts images or PDFs into machine-readable text.

## SIG
Prescription directions for use, often written in shorthand.

## NPI
National Provider Identifier. A public identifier for healthcare providers in the United States.

## RxNorm
A standardized drug terminology system from the U.S. National Library of Medicine.

## RxCUI
RxNorm Concept Unique Identifier. A unique ID for a drug concept in RxNorm.

## NDC
National Drug Code. A three-segment product identifier for drugs in the U.S.

## DailyMed
A public resource containing FDA label information submitted to the FDA.

## openFDA
FDA platform that provides public APIs for FDA datasets, including drug labels, NDC, adverse events, recalls, shortages, and Drugs@FDA.

## Drug Normalization
The process of converting messy medication text into a structured drug concept.

## Human-in-the-Loop
A system design where humans review or approve AI-generated outputs before final action.

## Pharmacist Supervisor
The human reviewer who approves, rejects, escalates, or corrects AI-generated prescription workflow outputs.

## Dispense Task
A task representing the simulated pick, count, package, label, and fulfillment process.

## Digital Twin
A visual simulation of the dispensing operation, such as shelves, bins, and robot task progress.

## Audit Log
An append-only record of system, AI, and human actions.

## Risk Level
A green/yellow/red classification used to indicate whether a prescription workflow appears routine, needs review, or requires escalation.

## Sandbox
A demo/test environment that does not use real patients, real prescriptions, or real dispensing.
